Defensive driving means anticipating potential hazards, staying alert, and making safe decisions to prevent accidents, even when others make mistakes.

Key Defensive Driving Tips

  • Stay Focused: Avoid distractions like phones, eating, or loud music. Keep your attention on the road at all times.
  • Safe Following Distance: Use the 3-second rule to keep a safe gap between you and the car ahead. Increase distance in bad weather.
  • Expect the Unexpected: Watch for sudden stops, lane changes, or unpredictable actions from other drivers, cyclists, and pedestrians.
  • Adjust for Conditions: Slow down in rain, fog, or heavy traffic. Be extra cautious at night or in construction zones.
  • Check Mirrors & Blind Spots: Scan your mirrors every 5-8 seconds and always check blind spots before changing lanes.
